2020 - 2023

Senior Data Associate @ Cancer Study Group

After graduating from University of Illinois, I joined a healthcare start-up based out of Washington D.C. called Cancer Study Group (CSG). CSG provides cancer research and navigational services to employees of corporations and unions with a focus on helping patients recieve high-quality cancer care. Here, I used delivered full-stack retrospective analyses to Fortune 500 employers and unions across the country. These analyses involved studying employees with cancer diagnoses and predicting gaps in their treatment journeys using personalized insights. Additionally, I conceptualized, built, and delivered the first-of-its-kind, comprehensive hospital-level volume analytics pipeline using the Fee-for-Service Medicare data. This process involved creating models to aggregate and adjust the Medicare data to represent the entire population of the United States and then mapping each facility and oncologist in the country to their respective cancer patient volumes. This mapping was then storyboarded into a dashboard which is serving as the foundation to help nurses steer cancer patients across the country to the best possible site of care. Technology Used: Python(Pandas, Numpy), MySQL, Tableau, AWS (RDS, EC2)

Projects
Genetic Chess Algorithm I created a program that was given the basic chess heuristics and “DNA” that tells each chess AI how to weigh moves. Then simulated evolution by having the AIs compete against each other and only allowed the winners to pass on their “genes.” In its current form through evolution, the AI can beat average human players.
Visualize-Sequel This project helps students practice SQL problems by breaking down their queries into smaller steps, and displaying the output tables fromeach step. This can guide students by helping them visualize what each step of their query does so they can adjust accordingly if their final result does not match the expected result. The number of steps is determined by their method of querying, which is described in the advanced function. From the instructor side, there is a question bankmanager, where they can modify, add or delete questions for students to practice and a student manager where they can alter and search the students that are signed up to the website.

Examining rates at which Esophageal Cancer patients reach High-Volume surgeons and Facilities. The key findings of this paper highlight the significant racial disparities that exist within minority populations seeking care for esophageal cancer. While 71.6% of White esophageal cancer patients reach high-volume surgeons, only 60% of Hispanic, 42.9% of Asian, and 41.9% of Black patients reach high-volume surgeons.
